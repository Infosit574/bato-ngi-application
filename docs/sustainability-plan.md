
# Sustainability Plan

The BATO project is designed for long-term sustainability across technical, financial, and organizational dimensions.

**Technical Sustainability**
- Open standards and modular architecture ensure independence from specific vendors.
- ActivityPub-ready schema enables future federation without migration.
- Comprehensive documentation supports knowledge transfer and community contributions.

**Financial Sustainability**
- Open-source core under AGPL v3 license.
- Commercial support and managed hosting services (Red Hat model):
  - Self-hosted: Free
  - Managed hosting: €50–200/month
  - Enterprise support: €500–2000/month

**Organizational Sustainability**
- Infosit commits to 24 months of post-project maintenance:
  - Repository upkeep
  - Issue triage
  - Security patches
  - Community moderation
- Formation of BATO Community starting Month 10 with contributor guidelines and quarterly calls.

**Environmental Sustainability**
- Offline-first architecture reduces dependency on energy-intensive cloud services.
- Dunbar-optimized RAG minimizes computational overhead (60–80% less than brute-force approaches).
- Low-resource deployment mode supports circular economy principles by enabling use of refurbished hardware.
